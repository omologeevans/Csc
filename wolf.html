Wolf

Wolves are highly intelligent and social carnivores, known for their pack behavior. They have thick fur coats, sharp teeth, and keen senses of smell and hearing. Packs of wolves work together to hunt large prey like deer and elk. They have a complex social structure, with an alpha male and female leading the group.

Physical Features:

  • Wolves have a compact, agile body covered in thick fur, which can be gray, white, or black.

  • Sharp teeth and powerful jaws are essential for hunting and eating prey.

Special Abilities:

  • Wolves have an exceptional sense of smell, capable of detecting scents from miles away, aiding in tracking prey and communicating with pack members.

  • They exhibit excellent teamwork within their packs, employing coordinated group efforts when hunting and defending territory.
