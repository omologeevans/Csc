Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates, divided into two species: eastern and western gorillas. They have muscular bodies, large hands, and are covered in black or brown hair.

Gorillas live in family groups called troops, led by a dominant silverback male. They are herbivores, mainly consuming plant material and fruits.

Physical Features:

  • Gorillas have a robust build with strong muscles, especially in males.

  • Their hair color ranges from black to brown, and they have a pronounced sagittal crest on their heads.

  • Large hands and feet are adapted for grasping and climbing.

Special Abilities:

  • Gorillas are known for their complex social structures, living in troops led by a dominant silverback male who maintains order and protection.

  • Communication involves a combination of vocalizations, body language, and facial expressions.
