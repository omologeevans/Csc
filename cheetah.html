Cheetah

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of sprinting at speeds up to 60-70 miles per hour in short bursts. They have slender bodies, tawny coats with distinctive black spots, and black "tear tracks" running from their eyes to their jaws.

Cheetahs are primarily solitary hunters, relying on their incredible speed and stealth to catch prey like antelope.

Physical Features:

  • Cheetahs have a slender, athletic build.

  • Their coat is tawny with black spots and "tear tracks" on their faces.

  • Non-retractable claws provide traction during high-speed chases.

Special Abilities:

  • Their speed and agility result in a high success rate in hunts, allowing them to chase down and capture prey with precision.

  • Cheetahs are renowned for their incredible speed, capable of accelerating from 0 to 60 miles per hour in just a few seconds.
