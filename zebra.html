Zebra

Zebras are known for their black and white striped coats, which serve as effective camouflage against predators such as lions and hyenas. Three species of zebras exist: plains zebras, mountain zebras, and Grevy's zebras.

They are herbivores, grazing on grasses in African grasslands, and often gather in groups for protection.

Physical Features:

  • Zebras possess a robust, horse-like build.

  • Their most striking feature is their black and white striped coat, which varies in pattern between individuals.

  • They have powerful hooves designed for running and delivering strong kicks.

Special Abilities:

  • The striking stripes may confuse biting flies, providing some protection against them.

  • A powerful kick serves as self-defense against predators like lions and hyenas.
