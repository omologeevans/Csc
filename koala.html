Koala

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia. They have round faces, fluffy ears, and a diet primarily consisting of eucalyptus leaves. These nocturnal animals are well adapted to tree-dwelling and spend most of their lives in eucalyptus trees.

Physical Features:

  • Koalas are small marsupials with a round face, fluffy ears, and a gray, soft fur.

  • Sharp claws on their hands and feet are essential for climbing and gripping tree branches.

Special Abilities:

  • Koalas are highly adapted to their diet of eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to many other animals. They can detoxify these leaves efficiently.

  • These nocturnal animals are well-suited to a tree-dwelling lifestyle.
